# AnUntitledFeesh - Story Outline

# Player Character

- The player is a tourist, visiting the island solely to fish and experience the adventure. They are always willing to help anyone who asks, embodying a friendly and helpful spirit.

- Character customization is available through a selection of hats and outfits, with new costumes relevant to each area. Locals offer these costumes for purchase, allowing the player to dress for the occasion and express their style.

# Premise

An eager fisher arrives on an island, having paid for "The Ultimate Fishing Adventure." Equipped with only a basic rod and tacklebox, they set out to explore, fish, and uncover the island's secrets.

## Main Story Arc

1. The player arrives at the island and is dropped off at the dock, beginning their adventure with basic gear.
2. Locals request specific fish in exchange for cash or information, introducing the player to fishing mechanics and the diversity of fish in different biomes (lakes, rivers, ponds, ocean, jungle/wetlands, cold mountain, volcano/lava).
3. The player explores each biome, catching new fish and progressing through increasingly challenging environments.
4. In each area, the player discovers a guardian's door that requires a "offering" of three fish. After the offering, the gate guard leads the player to the arena.
5. The selected fish spring to life and defend the player in a top-down bullet hell boss fight, each with unique weapons and stats.
6. After defeating the final area, the player is brought to the island headquarters, where it is revealed that the bosses were mechanical puppets controlled by locals to create the ultimate adventure.
7. The true nature of the fish fighting for the player is left mysterious, with clues hidden throughout the game for those seeking a secret ending.


## Key Characters

- "Fishing Adventure" Host: Greets the player upon arrival and at each new area, offering information about the locale and activities. The Host downplays or dismisses any mention of boss fights or magical fish, maintaining the illusion of a normal fishing vacation.

- Unique shopkeeper NPCs: Each area features its own shopkeeper, serving as a key character for that biome. They provide items, upgrades, and local flavor, and are important to the player's progression and the story of each region.

- The Gate Guard: A recurring character who appears at every offering altar across all biomes. The same gate guard guides the player through the sacrifice ritual and leads them to each boss arena, providing continuity and a sense of mystery.

### Area-Specific Characters

- **Forest - Bait Selling Woman**: Sells bait to the player, providing essential supplies for early fishing.

- **Forest - Farmer**: Offers a quirky side quest where the player plants a fish in the dirt, cares for it, and harvests a "fish plant" for a small reward.

- **Ocean - Boat Captain**: Provides transportation from the Ocean Biome to the Jungle/Wetlands by ferrying the player across the sea from the initial Forest island.

- **Jungle - Amazoness Fisher**: Introduces the option to fish with a bow, allowing the player to choose between bow or fishing pole while in the jungle.

- **Mountain - Mountain Climber**: Requests the player's help to reach the mountain summit by casting a line upward for the climber to shimmy up, rewarding the player for their assistance.

- **Lava - Scientist**: Studies the phenomenon of people fishing in lava and surviving the extreme heat, adding a touch of mystery and science to the area.


# Quest System

## Quest Types

### NPC Quests
- Locals display an exclamation mark above their heads when they have a quest available
- Quests typically request specific fish local to the area (e.g., "Bring me 3 Carp" or "Catch a Largemouth Bass")
- Rewards are modest amounts of cash - enough to buy bait restocks or a few upgrades
- All NPC quests are entirely optional and can be completed at the player's leisure
- Quests can be abandoned at any time if the player chooses not to complete them
- Turning in quest fish removes them from the player's inventory (the fish is given to the quest giver)

### Bounty Board Challenges
- Each area's shop features a bounty board with posted fishing challenges
- Bounties focus on catching fish with specific requirements: size, weight, quantity, or rarity (e.g., "Catch 5 fish over 10 lbs" or "Catch 3 Rare fish")
- Bounties are not traditional quests but posted challenges players can complete and turn in
- There is a limited number of bounties per area, tied to the number of rare and legendary fish in that biome
- Rewards include more cash than NPC quests, plus hints about how to catch rare and legendary fish
- As bounties are completed, players receive increasingly valuable information about legendary fish locations and catch conditions

## Quest Flow

### Discovery & Activation
- Each new area features an initial tutorial quest that provides information about local bait and fishing conditions
- After the tutorial quest, additional NPC quests become available from various locals throughout the area
- No quests are required for progression - the only requirement to advance is defeating the area's boss
- Players are free to skip all quests and rush to the boss if they choose

### Quest Tracking & UI
- Players can view all active quests and bounties in a quest log/journal menu
- Quest progress is displayed (e.g., "Caught 2/3 Carp" or "Caught fish weighing 4/10 lbs")
- Bounty board challenges appear separately from NPC quests for easy reference
- Players can abandon quests at any time without penalty

### Integration with Boss System
- There are no special "quest fish" - all fish caught can be used for quests, offerings, or kept in inventory
- The "offering of three fish" required at guardian doors is completely separate from quest fish
- Players must choose whether to turn in fish for quest rewards or save them for boss offerings
- Turning in fish for quests removes them from inventory permanently

## Area-Specific Mechanics
Each area introduces a unique fishing mechanic or environmental feature alongside its quests:
- **Jungle/Wetlands**: Bow fishing as an alternative to traditional rod fishing
- Additional area mechanics to be designed for other biomes


# Themes

- Adventure and discovery
- Mastery of fishing and strategy
- The thrill of challenge and overcoming obstacles
- Community, playfulness, and the joy of surprises
- Mystery and the search for hidden truths

- The world is humorous, over-the-top, and ridiculous, but all characters and systems take themselves completely seriously, creating comedy through earnestness and absurd situations.


# Story Progression Notes

- Each biome introduces new fishing mechanics, fish types, and environmental challenges.
- Boss fights are a major progression milestone, blending fishing with action gameplay.
- The "sacrifice" mechanic ties fishing choices to boss encounters, encouraging experimentation.
- The ending subverts expectations, revealing the adventure as a staged event, while leaving the magical element of the fish unexplained for deeper lore seekers.

# Collectibles / Progression Systems

- Each area features a bounty board in the shop, where players can get hints on how to catch legendary fish. This encourages exploration, experimentation, and interaction with the environment and NPCs.

# Recurring Motifs / Running Gags

- The world and its characters treat every absurd event with complete seriousness, no matter how ridiculous.
- The Host always downplays or ignores anything magical, dangerous, or outlandish, insisting it’s all part of the normal vacation package.
- NPCs frequently reference “island rules” or “fishing regulations” that are clearly made up on the spot.
- The Gate Guard’s mysteriousness is played for laughs—always appearing just before the player arrives, never explaining how they get there first.
- Bosses deliver over-the-top banter and dramatic monologues, but are never self-aware about being puppets.
- The player can collect increasingly silly hats and costumes, with locals acting as if each new outfit is perfectly normal attire for fishing.
- Side quests often involve nonsensical tasks (e.g., planting a fish, fishing in lava) that everyone treats as completely reasonable.

# Chapter / Area Summaries

## Forest
The adventure begins in a lush forest with a river, lake, and pond. The player meets friendly locals, learns the basics of fishing, and discovers the first guardians blocking passage to the wider island.

## Ocean
The player moves to a sunny beach and docks, gaining access to ocean fishing and boat travel. New fish, saltwater challenges, and quirky seafaring NPCs await.

## Jungle / Wetlands
Dense jungle and winding wetlands introduce new fishing techniques and hazards. The player encounters unique wildlife, tries bow fishing, and faces guardians with wild personalities.

## Mountain
The journey continues to a frigid mountain, where icy waters and treacherous terrain test the player’s skills. Locals and bosses here are tough and dramatic, and the environment is full of slapstick danger.

## Lava / Volcano
The final area is an active volcano, where the player fishes in lava and helps a scientist unravel the area’s mysteries. The guardians are the most over-the-top yet, and the truth behind the adventure draws near.

# Bosses

Each biome features 2 unique bosses ((adding replayability)), serving as the area guardian and the climax of that region's story and gameplay. Bosses are designed to be humorous, over-the-top, and memorable, with personalities and banter that fit the game's tone. Their true nature as mechanical puppets is revealed only at the end, but each boss is treated with complete seriousness by the world.
